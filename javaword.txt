font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2><div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg" class="image" title="Cascade Pass and Pelton Basin"><img alt="Cascade Pass and Pelton Basin" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg/120px-Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg" width="120" height="79" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg/180px-Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg/240px-Cascade_Pass_and_Pelton_Basin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3678" data-file-height="2418" /></a></div><p><b><a href="/wiki/North_Cascades_National_Park" title="North Cascades National Park">North Cascades National Park</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_the_United_States" title="List of national parks of the United States">US national park</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, established October 2, 1968. Covering more than 500,000 acres (200,000&#160;ha), it features the rugged mountain peaks of the <a href="/wiki/North_Cascades" title="North Cascades">North Cascades Range</a>, the most expansive glacial system in the <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">contiguous United States</a>, and vast forests with the highest degree of flora <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> of any US national park. It is bisected by the <a href="/wiki/Skagit_River" title="Skagit River">Skagit River</a>, which flows through <a href="/wiki/Ross_Lake_National_Recreation_Area" title="Ross Lake National Recreation Area">Ross Lake National Recreation Area</a>. The region was first settled by <a href="/wiki/Paleo-Indians" title="Paleo-Indians">Paleo-Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>; when white explorers and fur trappers arrived, <a href="/wiki/Skagit_peoples" title="Skagit peoples">Skagit tribes</a> lived there. In the 1920s several dams were built in the Skagit River valley to generate hydroelectric power. The park has one of the earliest and longest-lasting research programs dedicated to studying climate change, focusing on <a href="/wiki/Glacial_motion" title="Glacial motion">glacial retreat</a>. With almost all of the park protected as wilderness, there are few structures, roads or other improvements. Heavy snows and a high risk of <a href="/wiki/Avalanche" title="Avalanche">avalanches</a> severely limit visitation in the winter. data-file-height="5400" /></a></td><td style="padding:0 6px 0 0"><p><i><b><a href="/wiki/L%27Absinthe" title="L&#39;Absinthe">L'Absinthe</a></b></i>, also known as <i>Dans un Café</i>, was painted between 1875 and 1876 by French artist <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Degas" title="Edgar Degas">Edgar Degas</a>. The work portrays the lethargic and lonely figures of a woman and man with a glass of <a href="/wiki/Absinthe" title="Absinthe">absinthe</a> at <a href="/wiki/La_Nouvelle_Ath%C3%A8nes" title="La Nouvelle Athènes">La Nouvelle Athènes</a> in Paris.  The models are <a href="/wiki/Marcellin_Desboutin" title="Marcellin Desboutin">Marcellin Desboutin</a>, a painter and engraver, and <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Andr%C3%A9e" title="Ellen Andrée">Ellen Andrée</a>, an actress who also appeared in <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Manet" title="Édouard Manet">Manet</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Chez_le_p%C3%A8re_Lathuille" title="Chez le père Lathuille">Chez le père Lathuille</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Auguste_Renoir" title="Pierre-Auguste Renoir">Renoir</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Luncheon_of_the_Boating_Party" title="Luncheon of the Boating Party">Luncheon of the Boating Party</a></i>. The painting was derided by critics at its first two showings in 1876 and 1892, but sparked even greater controversy in an exhibition at the <a href="/wiki/Grafton_Gallery" class="mw-redirect" title="Grafton Gallery">Grafton Gallery</a> in England in 1893. The people and the absinthe represented in the painting were considered by English critics to be shockingly degraded and uncouth.
